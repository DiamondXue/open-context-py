Sponsors and Support

Open Context is maintained and administered by a dedicated staff with The Alexandria Archive Institute, a non-profit organization.The California Digital Library at the University of California provides data archiving and preservation services. Open Context development has been funded by foundation grants and charitable donations.

Financial Support for Open Context

The following organizations have provided financial support for Open Context's development and operations:

  • The National Endowment for the Humanities

    Supporting research and development around linked open data and user experience

  • The German Archaeological Institute

    Mirror hosting and back-up for Open Context

  • The J.M. Kaplan Fund

    Research and development on the integration of excavation and conservation data

  • The Encyclopedia of Life

    A Computable Data Challenge award for the Biogeography of Early Domestic Animals using Linked Open Data project

  • The National Science Foundation

    Support for the Digital Index of North American Archaeology project

  • The American Council of Learned Societies

    Support for research on data journals, data editing and publishing processes

  • The Alfred P. Sloan Foundation

    Support for data publishing workflows

  • The Institute of Museum and Library Services

    Support for user experience research

  • The William and Flora Hewlett Foundation

    Key institutional support in the development of Open Context

  • The Joukowsky Family Foundation

    Support for Open Context’s data publishing programs

  • The Leon Levy Foundation

    Support for Open Context’s data publishing programs
